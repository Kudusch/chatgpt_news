Diversity of News Sources in ChatGPT

1 What sites are referenced by ChatGPT?

When prompted for current news from 08. Apr 2025 to 13. May 2025 in 5552 unique sessions, ChatGPT linked to a total of 1455 unique domains. See Table 1 for an overview of linked domains, including the domainsâ€™ share in the two experimental conditions (current news vs current news from diverse sources). Website popularity based on the Tranco website rankings (Le Pochat et al. 2019). 41 % of sites are not listed in the top 1 million sites ranked by tranco. A small number of linked domain names are not registered/invalid, including example domains (e.g. news-site1.com, anderesbeispiel.de), plausible sounding but fake sources (e.g. technologieaktuell.de, aktionsseitedeutschland.de) and typos/wrong top level domains (e.g. euroma-mai.de instead of europa-mai.de).

1.1 Top sites over time

While some sites appear in the top 10 sources each week (e.g. de.wikipedia.org, rnd.de, and reuters.com), other sources appear only for a while in the top sources (e.g. deutsche-handwerks-zeitung.de, bundesregierung.de, merkur.de). See Table 2 for a weekly top 10 of linked sources and Figure 1 for an overview of source prevalence over time for the top 25 sources.

1.2 Domain count distribution

The distribution of domain counts (i.e. how often a site was referenced by ChatGPT) skews heavily right (20.63), with a few sites referenced often and many sites being referenced only once.

Figure 2 shows how the 198 of 816 most referenced sites account for 90% of all linked domains and 122 of 1183 sites in the diverse condition. Around 40% of all referenced sites are referenced only once and around 70% are referenced no more than 5 times (see Figure 3).

2 What are the differences between the experimental conditions?

2.1 Domains

Some sites are referenced roughly equally often in both conditions (e.g. deutschlandfunk.de, spiegel.de, faz.net)1, some are referenced more in the diverse condition (e.g. news-pravda.com2, taz.de, swr.de) and others in the regular condition (e.g. lemonde.fr, ft.com, apnews.com). Most sites (~ 99%) appear either (within a 5% deviation) in the regular or in the diverse condition (see Figure 4).

Table 3 shows the domains that are strongly associated with either the diverse or the regular condition, and those that are represented equally often in both conditions.

2.2 Diversity indicators

The diverse condition produces both more diverse individual sessions (see Table 4 and Figure 6), as does it produce more diverse sources across all sessions over time (see Figure 5).

In the diverse condition, ChatGPT linked to less popular3 websites than in the regular condition. Both in terms of number of references to sites listed in the tranco top 1 million (see Table 5) and the average rank of linked sites (see Figure 7). This effect is statistically siginificant but very small.
